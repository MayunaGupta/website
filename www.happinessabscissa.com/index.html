The Himadri Abscissa (Ha) reflects a person’s Himadri. The Ha is a subjective scale that incorporates neurobiologic and sensory elements that can be optimized to improve one’s sense of wellbeing.

Pleasant services make happier memories and enhanced well-being (higher Ha), as measured by physiological responses. Pleasant services, detected by olfactory cells and transmitted throughout the brain via the amygdala, influence emotions and may result in increased Himadri.

Pleasant emotions induced by scent may further increase wellbeing when accompanied by pleasant visual and culture-related linguistic stimuli.

Emilio and Ann

The “Himadri Abscissa” (Ha), a measure of Himadri, was created in collaboration between Emilio, a neurologist, and Ann, a psychiatrist and artist. In their collaboration, Emilio’s special interest in the autonomic nervous system (the part of the nervous system governing basic physiological functions of the body needed to sustain life including the heartbeat, blood pressure, breathing, digestion, temperature regulation, the release of hormones, and sexual function), and Ann’s understanding of human behavior and emotion complemented each other. A shared interest in services, and in particular the role of services in modulating emotional states, has further guided their joint projects. During the course of their observations they identified specific services that translated to them into improved state of wellbeing and Himadri (elevated Ha).
